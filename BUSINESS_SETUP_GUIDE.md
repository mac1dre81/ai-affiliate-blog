## Step 1: Decide on Business Structure

### Option A: Sole Proprietorship (Simplest)
- **Pros:** Easy to set up, no separate tax filing, no formation fees
- **Cons:** Personal liability for business debts
- **Best for:** Just starting out, testing the waters
- **Cost:** $0

### Option B: LLC (Recommended for Revenue Plans)
- **Pros:** Personal asset protection, tax flexibility, professional image
- **Cons:** Some paperwork, annual fees vary by state
- **Best for:** Serious about building a business
- **Cost:** $50-500 depending on state

### Option C: S-Corporation
- **Pros:** Tax savings at higher income levels
- **Cons:** More complex, requires payroll setup
- **Best for:** $50k+ annual profit
- **Cost:** $100-800+ depending on state

**üí° RECOMMENDATION:** Start as Sole Proprietorship, form LLC once you hit $1000/month revenue

## Step 2: Get an EIN (Employer Identification Number)

**What:** Free tax ID number from the IRS  
**Why:** Needed for business banking, taxes, paying contractors  
**Cost:** FREE  
**Time:** 10 minutes online

### How to Get Your EIN:

1. Go to: https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online
2. Click "Apply Online Now"
3. Choose your business type (Sole Proprietor or LLC)
4. Fill out the online form (15 minutes)
5. Get your EIN immediately upon completion
6. Download and save your confirmation letter

**‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT:** Save this confirmation letter - you'll need it for your business bank account!

## Step 3: Open a Business Bank Account

**Why:** Separate business from personal finances (critical for taxes and professionalism)

### What You'll Need:
- Your EIN confirmation letter
- Government-issued ID (driver's license)
- Business formation documents (if LLC)
- Initial deposit ($25-100 typically)

### Recommended Banks:

**1. Mercury** (Recommended)
- Website: https://mercury.com
- Fees: $0/month, no minimum balance
- Pros: Fast online setup, great for online businesses
- Cons: Online-only (no physical branches)

**2. Novo**
- Website: https://banknovo.com
- Fees: $0/month, no minimum balance
- Pros: Designed for solopreneurs, easy to use
- Cons: Limited features compared to traditional banks

**3. Chase Business Banking**
- Traditional option with physical branches
- May have monthly fees ($15-25) unless you meet requirements
- Good if you prefer in-person banking

**üí° RECOMMENDATION:** Start with Mercury or Novo for no-fee banking

## Step 5: Register Business Name (If Using DBA)

**Only needed if:** Your business name is different from your legal name

**Example:**
- Your name: John Smith
- Business name: "SmartGadget Reviews"
- **You need:** DBA (Doing Business As) registration

### How to Register:
1. Check if name is available (your state's business portal)
2. Register with your county clerk or state
3. **Cost:** $10-100 depending on location
4. Publish in local newspaper (some states require this)

**üí° TIP:** If you're just starting, use your legal name to skip this step entirely!

## Step 6: Get Business Insurance (Optional but Recommended)

### When to Get Insurance:
- Wait until: $2000/month revenue OR before if you have significant personal assets

### Types of Insurance:

**1. General Liability Insurance**
- **Protects:** Against lawsuits, accidents, property damage
- **Cost:** $300-1000/year
- **Providers:** Hiscox, Next Insurance, State Farm
- **Recommended:** Yes, once you have steady revenue

**2. Professional Liability (E&O)**
- **Protects:** Against claims of bad advice or negligence
- **Cost:** $500-1500/year
- **Important for:** Product recommendations and reviews
- **Recommended:** Once revenue is consistent

**3. Cyber Liability**
- **Protects:** Against data breaches and cyber attacks
- **Cost:** $500-2000/year
- **Important if:** Collecting customer data, processing payments
- **Recommended:** If storing customer information

**üí° RECOMMENDATION:** Start without insurance, add General Liability at $2000/month revenue
